Janam Kundli

Discover Your Destiny Through the Blueprint of the Stars – Unlock Life’s Secrets with Janam Kundli.
About

A Janam Kundli, also known as a birth chart or horoscope, is a detailed astrological diagram that maps the exact positions of celestial bodies at the time of your birth. Based on your date, time, and place of birth, this chart provides a personalized blueprint of your life's path, personality traits, strengths, weaknesses, and potential milestones.
In Vedic astrology, the Kundli is a powerful tool used to understand one’s life journey. It showcases the placement of planets across 12 houses and zodiac signs, each influencing key areas such as career, marriage, finances, and health. Astrologers use these planetary alignments to reveal patterns, offering guidance and clarity for life’s challenges and decisions.
A Janam Kundli is also essential for making key predictions like dasha periods, transits (Gochar), and Muhurat (auspicious timings), making it a valuable guide throughout life.

Detailed Insights from Janam Kundli

A Janam Kundli is divided into 12 houses, and each house governs a unique domain of an individual's life. At the time of birth, the zodiac sign rising on the eastern horizon becomes the first house, and the remaining signs follow sequentially. The placement of planets in these houses reveals deep insights about a person’s nature, relationships, career, health, and more.

Here’s what each of the 12 houses signifies

  • First House (Tanu Bhav): Represents the self, personality, physical body, and overall appearance.
  • Second House (Dhana Bhav): Linked to wealth, family, speech, and accumulated resources.
  • Third House (Sahaj Bhav): Indicates courage, communication skills, younger siblings, and short travels.
  • Fourth House (Sukh Bhav): Governs home life, emotional well-being, mother, and real estate.
  • Fifth House (Putra Bhav): Reflects creativity, children, education, and romantic inclinations.
  • Sixth House (Shatru Bhav): Represents health, competition, enemies, debts, and service.
  • Seventh House (Kalatra Bhav): Relates to marriage, partnerships, and legal agreements.
  • Eighth House (Mrityu Bhav): Indicates transformation, longevity, occult, and hidden matters.
  • Ninth House (Dharma Bhav): Concerns luck, higher education, religion, ethics, and long journeys.
  • Tenth House (Karma Bhav): Signifies career, reputation, professional achievements, and public image.
  • Eleventh House (Labha Bhav): Represents gains, income, ambitions, and social networks.
  • Twelfth House (Vyaya Bhav): Associated with expenses, losses, spirituality, isolation, and foreign travels.

The strength of each house and its ruling planet can significantly impact life decisions, which is why Kundli analysis is vital before major milestones like marriage, job change, or investment.
